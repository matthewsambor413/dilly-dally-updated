## BRAND STORY
## AUGUST 2025
## NEW YORK
A fair organized by a maker, for makers — and for the people who love what makers do.
Scarlett was applying to a craft fair she was pretty sure would reject her.
The fees were steep. The application was competitive. And as someone who spends her weeks on building
facades and her weekends at the pottery wheel, she didn't exactly have the time or the résumé to fight for
a spot at a table she wasn't sure she'd even feel welcome at.
So somewhere in the middle of filling out the application, she thought — I'll just make my own.
Not out of bitterness. More like a shrug that turned into something. If the door is hard to find, maybe
just leave yours open. If the table is hard to get to, maybe just make your own.
Dilly Dally Fair is what came out of that shrug. A fair organized by a maker, for makers — people with full
time jobs and part time obsessions, people mid-process, people who make things slowly and carefully and
aren't sure yet where it's all going. There are no steep entry fees. The application doesn't ask for your
credentials. It asks what you're currently figuring out.
And for visitors, it's an invitation to wander without agenda — to find a sculptor who clocks out at six, a
scent maker who melts wax in her kitchen after dinner, a furniture maker who spends his days trapped in
excel models and his nights arguing with wood. The best things here won't be on a website yet. Some of them
aren't even finished.
Come dilly dally.
